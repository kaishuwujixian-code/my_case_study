Project Overview

The Joyce Road solar installation is located at 576 Joyce Rd, Stone Mills, Ontario. The site receives approximately 1290 peak-sun-hours per year and was developed under Ontario’s Feed-in-Tariff program as a grid-connected photovoltaic asset. Mann Engineering was engaged on the project for design and project management, and the site later appears within the operating and O&M records you provided. The project summary identifies a total system size of 111 kWp DC with a maximum output of approximately 90 kW AC. Based on the updated Joyce Farm operating workbook, the site produced 119,635 kWh over the July 2023 to June 2024 period.

The system consists of 396 Suntech 280 W modules, 18 KACO 5002xi inverters, and 9 Eaton combiner boxes. These components create a distributed inverter architecture that supports practical maintenance and limits single-point operational risk.

Technical Fit

Joyce Road combines strong solar availability, usable roof area, and a practical interconnection path. That makes it an ideal medium-scale rooftop solar story: the design is clear, the equipment is easy to explain, and the system performance can be supported by operating data rather than theory alone.

Commercial and Environmental Value

Joyce Road is a particularly strong promotional case because the project can be supported with actual generation and revenue records, while also reflecting Mann Engineering’s role in design, project management, and the longer operating story captured in the O&M materials you provided. Over the July 2023 to June 2024 period, the system generated 119,635 kWh and produced $75,968.53 in gross revenue, or $66,596.95 net after listed charges. This allows the project to be described as a real producing asset rather than only a completed installation.

On the environmental side, this level of annual solar generation represents roughly 10.8 tonnes of CO₂e avoided per year when using a 90 gCO₂e/kWh Ontario-era grid factor. That strengthens the case study for clients who care about both carbon reduction and long-term operating economics.
